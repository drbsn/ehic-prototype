Get healthcare cover for travelling abroad

Use this service to apply for an EHIC (European Health Insurance Card)

An EHIC gives you the right to access state-provided healthcare on temporary stays in countries in the European Union (EU), European Economic Area (EEA) and Switzerland.

Countries in the EU and EEA
EU countries

The EU countries are:

Austria, Belgium, Bulgaria, Croatia, Republic of Cyprus, Czech Republic, Denmark, Estonia, Finland, France, Germany, Greece, Hungary, Ireland, Italy, Latvia, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Malta, Netherlands, Poland, Portugal, Romania, Slovakia, Slovenia, Spain and Sweden.


The European Economic Area (EEA)

The EEA includes EU countries and also Iceland, Liechtenstein and Norway. It allows them to be part of the EUâ€™s single market.

Switzerland is not an EU or EEA member but is part of the single market. This means Swiss nationals have the same rights to live and work in the UK as other EEA nationals.


You can get an EHIC if:
  • you are a UK citizen
  • you are an EU, EEA or Swiss citizen with either settled or pre-settled status in the UK or you have indefinite leave to remain in the UK
  • you are a posted worker living and working in the EU, EEA or Switzerland
  • you are a UK citizen studying in the EU, EEA or Switzerland
  • you receive a UK State Pension or other exportable benefit and are living in the EU, EEA or Switzerland
