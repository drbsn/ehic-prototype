If you are living in the EU, Norway, Iceland, Lichtenstein, or Switzerland, an S1, E121, E106 or E109 certificate entitles you to access the same state-provided healthcare cover as a citizen of that country.

You might be entitled to an S1 certificate (formerly known as an E121 certificate) if you live in the EU, Norway, Iceland, Lichtenstein, or Switzerland and receive a UK State Pension, or are in receipt of a qualifying benefit.

You might be entitled to an S1 or E106 certificate if you live in the EU, Norway, Iceland, Lichtenstein, or Switzerland and work in the UK.

You might also be entitled if you are the family member of someone who has been awarded an S1, E121 or E106 certificate.

If you have been provided with a UK S1, E121, E106 or E109 certificate, you will have been asked to register it with your local health authority.

If you have not registered your certificate, you will need to do so before you can apply for cover.

If you have not been supplied with an S1, E121, E106 or E109 certificate, or you have not yet registered it, answer no.
