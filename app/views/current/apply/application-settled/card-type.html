Choose the cover you wish to apply for

Based on the information you have provided you may be entitled to a UK Global Health Insurance Card (GHIC) or new UK European Health Insurance Card (EHIC).

What is the difference?

A UK GHIC covers you when visiting an EU country and Switzerland. A UK GHIC may become valid in more countries in the future.

A new UK EHIC covers you when visiting an EU country or Switzerland, as well as Norway, Iceland or Liechtenstein.

If you plan to visit Norway, Iceland or Liechtenstein you may wish to apply for a new UK EHIC.

Which countries are in the EU?

The EU countries are:

Austria, Belgium, Bulgaria, Croatia, Republic of Cyprus, Czech Republic, Denmark, Estonia, Finland, France, Germany, Greece, Hungary, Ireland, Italy, Latvia, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Malta, Netherlands, Poland, Portugal, Romania, Slovakia, Slovenia, Spain and Sweden.

Information for overseas students

If you're a student studying in the EU or Switzerland:

  • both a UK GHIC and a new UK EHIC will cover you for necessary state-provided healthcare within the EU and Switzerland, including your country of study. You will not be able to use either card for treatment in Norway, Iceland, or Liechtenstein.

If you're a student studying in Norway, Iceland, or Liechtenstein:

  • a new UK EHIC will cover you for necessary state-provided healthcare in your country of study and when visiting EU countries or Switzerland
  • a UK GHIC will cover you for necessary state-provided healthcare when visiting the EU or Switzerland. You will not be able to use your card for treatment in your country of study
Healthcare cover in Switzerland

Your card only covers you for healthcare in Switzerland if you're:

  • a UK national
  • a Swiss national
  • a national of an EU country
  • a stateless person or refugee
  • a family member of someone who holds one of these nationalities or statuses.

You may be asked for proof of nationality or status when accessing healthcare in Switzerland.
